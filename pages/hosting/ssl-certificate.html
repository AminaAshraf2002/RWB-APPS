SSL Certificate & Installation

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) Certificates are globally recognized security certificates that secure websites. Also known as Digital Certificates, they create an encrypted communication link between a web server and a browser. When a website is SSL certified, it shows a lock symbol in the browser, ensuring secure communication between the website and its visitors.

What is SSL Certificate?

SSL Secure Socket Layer SSL is the standard security technology used to establish an encrypted link between a web server and a browser. This encrypted link ensures that all data passed between them remains private, secure, and safe.

What is HTTPS?

HTTPS Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) is a secure version of HTTP. When using HTTPS, computers agree on a shared "code" to scramble messages. This makes the communication unreadable to anyone trying to intercept it, thus keeping your information safe from hackers.

Mark your site “Not Secure”

Today, Google strongly encourages implementing SSL as the best practice for secure websites. Not having SSL could not only jeopardize your website’s security but also result in lost revenue. Websites without SSL certification are flagged as "Not Secure," which could deter users from visiting and engaging with the site.

What are the impacts of not having SSL?

As more online businesses transition to secure websites, SSL certification will become a necessary standard for success. Websites lacking SSL will be viewed as less trustworthy, potentially losing customers who prefer to engage only with secure sites. Having an SSL certificate is essential for building trust and achieving long-term online success.

Types of SSL Certificates

EV SSL Certificate

EV SSL Certificates are extended validation certificates, the most secure among the three types of SSL certificates. They involve a stringent vetting process to validate and verify the legitimacy of the entity requesting the certificate. When a website is secured with an authorized EV SSL Certificate, users will see a green address bar in their browser. This green address bar helps build customer trust, enhancing credibility and significantly increasing conversion rates.

OV SSL Certificate

OV (Organization Validated) SSL Certificates provide full business and company validation. Each OV certificate includes the company’s name and address details, offering a higher level of trust to users. Compared to Domain Validated certificates, OV SSL certificates provide significantly greater assurance to end-users, ensuring the legitimacy of the organization behind the website.

DV SSL Certificate

DV (Domain Validated) SSL Certificates provide the same high levels of data encryption as the other types, but they do not display the green address bar and do not offer assurance about the identity of the business behind the website. These certificates are a popular choice for small or startup websites due to their faster issuance times and lower price points, making them an affordable option for basic website security.
